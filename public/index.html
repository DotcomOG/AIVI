What AIVI is — and is not

AIVI is an observational index. It measures how AI systems interpret and represent a website based on observable signals.

  • Not an SEO tool — no keywords, backlinks, or rank tracking
  • Not a content grader — it observes interpretability, not style preference
  • Not a prompt test — it evaluates persistent signals, not a single-model output
  • Not a checklist — it identifies stability and risk, not to-do lists
  • Not a guarantee engine — it does not promise outcomes, rankings, or citations
AIVI does not tell you what to do. It shows you what AI already believes.

Technical Whitepaper: AI Visibility Index (AIVI) Framework ▾

Executive Summary

Search is changing faster than at any time in the past 20 years. AI systems now provide direct answers instead of listing links. As these models become primary gateways to information, businesses face a new challenge: they must be visible not only to search engines but to AI itself. The AI Visibility Index (AIVI) is a measurement system built to understand how AI systems perceive, interpret, trust, and surface a website. Instead of optimizing for ranking signals designed for legacy search engines, AIVI measures how modern AI models evaluate content, structure, authority, and language.

Background and Problem Statement

Traditional SEO practices were built for keyword matching and link-based authority systems. AI answer engines behave differently: they synthesize, compress, and generalize. This creates a visibility gap — companies often can’t see how AI interprets them, or why competitors appear more frequently in generated answers.

Introducing AIVI

AIVI analyzes a website across four core pillars that influence how AI systems understand and trust content: Structure and Crawlability, Authority and Trust, Clarity and Language, and Content–AI Alignment. The result is an index reading that helps teams understand perception, credibility, and representation risk.

The AIVI Report

  • AIVI index reading
  • Visibility Tier classification
  • Summary of how AI currently interprets the site
  • Key weaknesses that reduce AI visibility
  • Prioritized recommendations (where applicable)
  • Representation risk signals
